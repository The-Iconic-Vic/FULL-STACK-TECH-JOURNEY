Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

⭐ Rating: 4.9/5 (1,234 reviews) | ⏱️ Time: 30 mins | 🍽️ Serves: 24 cookies

The perfect chocolate chip cookie recipe that's crispy on the edges and chewy in the middle. Passed down through generations and foolproof every time!

📖 Why This Recipe Works

These cookies have a perfect balance of crispy edges and chewy centers. The secret is melting the butter instead of creaming it, which creates a denser, more flavorful cookie. This recipe has been tested over 100 times to get the proportions just right.

📌 First time baking? This recipe is beginner-friendly and almost impossible to mess up!

🛒 Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, melted
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Equipment You'll Need

  • Large mixing bowls (2)
  • Electric mixer or whisk
  • Baking sheets
  • Parchment paper
  • Cookie scoop or spoon

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Mix dry ingredients. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Combine wet ingredients. In a large bowl, beat melted butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  4. Add eggs and vanilla. Beat in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla extract.
  5. Combine wet and dry. Gradually add flour mixture to wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Do not overmix!
  6. Add chocolate chips. Stir in chocolate chips and walnuts (if using).
  7. Scoop cookies. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto prepared baking sheets, spacing about 2 inches apart.
  8. Bake for 9-11 minutes or until edges are golden brown. Centers will look slightly underbaked.
  9. Cool for 5 minutes on baking sheets, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

💡 Pro Tips for Perfect Cookies

  • Chill the dough for 24-48 hours for deeper flavor and thicker cookies.
  • Use a cookie scoop for uniform cookies that bake evenly.
  • Tap the baking sheet on the counter halfway through baking for wrinkled, crispy edges.
  • Store with a slice of bread to keep cookies soft longer.
